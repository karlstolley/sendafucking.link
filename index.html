Send a Fucking Link

No one wants your 27GB email attachment, and no one wants your easily exploitable third-party share message. Send a fucking link. That old-school monster that opens with http:// or https:// that you copy and paste into an email yourself.

Plenty of services enable share links, which are accessible even to people who do not use your file-storage service of choice. Here are instructions and examples for Dropbox and Google Drive. Use something else? Please add it.

Send a Fucking Dropbox Share Link

If you have Dropbox installed on your desktop, just right-click the file or folder in your Dropbox folder that you want to share and choose Get share link.... That will copy a custom link to your clipboard, ready to insert into your email message or whatever.

If you’re using the Dropbox website, find the file or folder in your account, hit the Share button, and choose the tiny little Create link link. Then choose Show link and copy it to your clipboard.

When you’re done, you’ll have a link that looks like this: https://www.dropbox.com/s/oglepbxviww1snb/shared-file.txt?dl=0 Pro tip: before you send your link, test it in your browser’s private browsing mode. That’s what a non-logged-in, non-you recipient of the link will see.

Full Dropbox share instructions are at https://www.dropbox.com/help/files-folders/view-only-access. For the platform-specific instructions, pay attention only to the instructions for Share a link, not share a file.

Send a Fucking Google Drive Share Link

From the Google Drive website, find the file or folder in your account, right-click it, and choose Get shareable link and copy the URL that shows up in the little Link Sharing box next to your file.

When you’re done, you’ll have a link that looks like this: https://drive.google.com/open?id=0B8emCiPoFkZ0ZU94WHVZekE1YzA Pro tip: before you send your link, test it in your browser’s private browsing mode. That’s what a non-logged-in, non-you recipient of the link will see.

Full Google Drive share instructions are at https://support.google.com/docs/answer/2494822. Pay attention only to the instructions for Anyone with a link to the file.

Send a Fucking Firefox Send Link

Just need a one time link or protect your privacy? Fine, use Firefox Send. Select/Drag your file in the box and you will get a fucking link. The link will automatically expires after the recipient download it or after 24 hours to ensure your stuff does not remain online forever.

When you’re done, you’ll have a link that looks like this: https://send.firefox.com/download/04ae6b0542/#m9zS4J3xkgA1ib0ROT2s5Q Pro tip: You do not need go to private browsing mode and test it, just copy and paste the link in your email message or whatever.
